# 📊 Excel Data Analysis 

## 🧠 Key Skills Demonstrated

### 🔢 Core Excel Formulas

Used throughout the workbook to calculate totals, averages, and derive new fields:

| Formula | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| `SUM()` | Calculated total commission values |
| `AVERAGE()` | Found average commission values |
| `SUMIF()` / `SUMIFS()` | Summed quantities by age category and other conditions |
| `AVERAGEIF()` | Calculated conditional averages |
| `DATE()`, `MONTH()`, `YEAR()` | Extracted date components for time‑based analysis |
| `UNIQUE()` | Identified distinct values in datasets |
| `VLOOKUP()` | Looked up related information across tables |

---

### 🔍 Filtering & Sorting

- Sorted the **Age** column from *largest to smallest* to explore demographic patterns.  
- Applied filters to isolate specific customer groups and product categories.

### 📊 PivotTables

Created multiple PivotTables to summarise complex datasets:

- **Retail sales**: summarised commissions, quantities, and demographics  
- **Bike sales**: analysed profitability by country, age group, and gender  
  - Example findings:  
    - Germany’s customers were in the *Adults (35–64)* group  
    - UK & Australia had sales in all markets  
- **County‑level product sales**: summarised sales volume by county and product type

PivotTables revealed trends such as:

- Higher bike sales in older age categories  
- Regional differences in product demand

Used conditional logic to classify products by sales volume (e.g., High, Medium, Low) based on thresholds defined in the dataset.

### 🎨 Charts & Visualisations

Created charts from the Bike Sales Visualisation Lab to explore:

## 📈 Insights Gained

- Data becomes far more meaningful when summarised through PivotTables.  
- Conditional formulas (SUMIFS, AVERAGEIF) are powerful for multi‑criteria analysis.  
- Categorisation logic simplifies grouping and interpretation of raw values.  
- Visualisations reveal patterns not immediately obvious in raw tables.  
- Sorting and filtering are foundational but essential for quick insights.
